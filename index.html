Who We are

The Application Development Initiative (ADI) seeks to nurture student creativity and technological aptitude by creating a community and resources for student developers of all talents and interests.

What We Do

ADI tries to cultivate a culture of learning and innovatin among students at Columbia. We want students to feel empowered to build anything, and hold events to help them in the process:

  • Every spring, ADI hosts DevFest, a week-long series of technical workshops culminating in a 24-hour hackathon. DevFest focuses on fostering community, and helping new developers end the week with a completed project.
  • ADI coordinates a communal, on-campus living space, where students interested in technology and innovation live together and learn from one another.
  • Every Wednesday night, ADI hosts Cookies & Code, where student developers meet one another, share feedback on code, and eat cookies.
  • ADI hosts a yearly job fair in the fall for student developers to connect with New York tech startups in a low-key and casual environment. Last year we hosted twenty companies and over 250 students.
  • ADI hosts weekly workshops and tech talks, bringing professional developers to campus to teach students their expertise. In addition, ADI hosts student-run talks, during which students teach each other skills they have developed.

ADI hosts and manages technical projects for the Columbia community.

  • ADI curates learn.adicu.com, a resources page for people interested in learning to program.
  • Students in ADI build courses.adicu.com, a schedule builder for Columbia students.
  • ADI built data.adicu.com, an API for accessing Columbia's data, from housing to dining to athletics.

Get Involved

Interested in getting involved? To get a taste of ADI, come to Cookies & Code on Wednesday night for an opportunity to talk to community members and work in a collaborative environment. Sign up for our newsletter to learn about weekly workshops and events.
